RAD-AI: Rethinking Architecture Documentation for AI-Augmented Ecosystems

Authors: Oliver Aleksander Larsen, Mahyar T. Moghaddam

Venue: ANGE 2026 Workshop at IEEE ICSA 2026

Status: Accepted — to appear in proceedings after the conference (June 22–26, 2026, Amsterdam)

Abstract

We propose a backward-compatible extension of arc42 (adding 8 sections) and C4 (adding 3 diagram types) specifically designed for AI-augmented ecosystems. Our extensions enable systematic documentation of AI components, their data flows, training pipelines, and regulatory requirements. When evaluated against EU AI Act Annex IV requirements, RAD-AI improves documentation addressability from approximately 36% to 93%. We validate the framework on two industrial case studies: Uber Michelangelo and Netflix Metaflow.

TL;DR

We extended two popular architecture documentation frameworks (arc42 and C4) to handle AI systems, improving EU AI Act compliance coverage from ~36% to ~93%.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best architecture documentation framework for AI systems?

RAD-AI extends arc42 and C4 — two of the most widely adopted frameworks — with AI-specific sections and diagrams, making it backward-compatible with existing documentation practices.

How does RAD-AI address EU AI Act compliance?

RAD-AI maps its documentation sections directly to EU AI Act Annex IV requirements, improving addressability from ~36% (vanilla arc42/C4) to ~93%.

What case studies validate RAD-AI?

We evaluated RAD-AI on two production ML platforms: Uber Michelangelo and Netflix Metaflow, demonstrating practical applicability at scale.
