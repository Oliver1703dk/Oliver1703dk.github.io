Hybrid Large-Language-Model Meal Recommender System for Athletes: A Short Communication

Authors: Oliver Aleksander Larsen, Vinay Chakravarthi Gogineni

Venue: Results in Engineering, Under Revision

Status: Accepted — to appear in proceedings after the conference (June 22–26, 2026, Amsterdam)

Abstract

We present a hybrid meal recommender system for athletes that combines fine-tuned large language models with nutritional constraint satisfaction. The system uses LoRA/PEFT-adapted Mistral-7B-Instruct to generate personalized meal recommendations that account for dietary requirements, macronutrient targets, and athletic performance goals. Evaluated on dietary-aware meal recommendation tasks, the system achieves 46.7% accuracy with 100% format adherence, demonstrating the viability of parameter-efficient fine-tuning for structured nutrition planning. Extended from a BSc thesis at the University of Southern Denmark.

Frequently Asked Questions

What model does the meal recommender use?

The system uses Mistral-7B-Instruct fine-tuned with LoRA/PEFT (Parameter-Efficient Fine-Tuning), enabling dietary-aware meal generation without full model retraining.

How accurate is the meal recommender?

The system achieves 46.7% accuracy on meal recommendation tasks with 100% format adherence, meaning every generated meal plan follows the required nutritional structure.

Is this related to CarboPlanner?

This research extends the BSc thesis work at SDU on personalized nutrition for athletes, which informed the development of the CarboPlanner startup.
